About Somewhere in Africa company

Alan, a Tanzanian businessman, tour guide, and entrepreneur, established Somewhere in Africa in 2010. Our English-speaking professional safari and travel guides provide one-of-a-kind travel experiences for memories that last a lifetime. We offer specialized Tanzanian private tours.

Our Purpose

Our goal has remained constant since the beginning: to give visitors the best possible impression of East Africa. Everything about traveling to East Africa is affected by this. From custom East Africa travel plans that satisfy all requirements to the five-star lodges and safari accommodations provided on East Africa specialist trips, everything is top-notch. In fact, our on-site team of experts personally evaluates over 300 hotels and lodges throughout East Africa that we partner with.

Our Tale

Alan fell in love with Tanzania and East Africa safaris after just one unforgettable tour, which was the result of a passion for authentic, safe travel in East Africa. Alan teamed up with one of Tanzania's top safari and luxury travel guides after spotting a significant gap in the market for high-quality, private tour companies there. Over the following few years, somewhere in Africa brought in thousands of customers from all over the world to its verdant landscapes. The business now has 14 full-time employees in Arusha

Our Safari Vehicles

7-seater custom safari vehicles are part of our own fleet. The best sliding windows and thatched roofs for viewing wildlife, sightseeing, and taking expert photos are included in each unit. These are all essential for an unforgettable safari tour. Before leaving, vehicles are cleaned and sanitized, and a qualified mechanic performs routine maintenance to guarantee safety and security. Each passenger has a window seat, and there is enough room to move around without feeling cramped. When watching games from the vehicle for up to 10 hours a day, this is essential.Your mode of transportation will be crucial wherever you go in Africa. On safaris, a wide variety of vehicles are used. You can find descriptions of the vehicles used in each location on your personalized itinerary. The safari vehicles can be closed with photographic hatches or open on the sides, depending on where you're going. To ensure maximum comfort and that every visitor has a clear view of the wildlife and scenery, Somewhere in Africa does not use overcrowding in its vehicles.
