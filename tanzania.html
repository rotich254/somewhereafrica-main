Tanzania Accommodations

Tanzania offers diverse accommodation options to suit the preferences and budgets of travelers. Safari lodges and tented camps situated within the country's national parks and conservation areas provide an immersive wildlife experience, often with luxurious amenities. Zanzibar, an island off Tanzania's coast, offers exquisite beachfront resorts with stunning ocean views. In cities like Arusha.

Arusha Coffee Lodge

The Manor at Ngorongoro is a 1500-acre coffee estate adjacent to the Ngorongoro National Park. It offers warm hospitality and beautiful accommodation.The elegant Cape-Dutch style manor has 10 guest cottages classically decorated with a European flavor. Each room has a private sun terrace, fireplace, rainfall shower and Victorian ball and claw bath. Small groups can book neighboring cottages or simply book the three-bedroom cottage.

Chem Chem Lodge

Chem Chem Lodge is situated within a privileged Wildlife Management Area between Tarangire National Park and Lake Manyara known as the Burunge Wildlife Management Area. The Lodge itself can be found nestled on the picturesque western side of the wildlife concession and consists of eight secluded tent-style suites with an opulent main Lodge house, all evoking the spirit of a vintage safari lodge.

FAQ's About Tanzania

As you plan your journey to this East African gem, you might have questions about what to expect, where to go, and how to make the most of your visit. In this FAQ guide, we've gathered answers to common inquiries that will help you explore Tanzania with confidence and excitement.

1.What is the best time to visit Tanzania and Zanzibar?

The best time for a safari in Tanzania and Zanzibar depends on the specific parks and activities you plan to engage in. Generally, the dry season from June to October is popular for wildlife viewing in Tanzania, while the months of December to February offer pleasant weather and are suitable for beach activities in Zanzibar. However, it's important to note that Tanzania and Zanzibar have year-round safari and beach options, so it's best to consult with your tour operator based on your preferences.Check our travel guide for more

2.What are the popular Parks in Tanzania for Safaris

Tanzania is renowned for its national parks, offering diverse wildlife and stunning landscapes. Some popular parks for safaris include Serengeti National Park, Ngorongoro Conservation Area, Tarangire National Park, Lake Manyara National Park, Selous Game Reserve, and Ruaha National Park. Each park offers unique experiences and wildlife encounters.

3.Can I combine a Safari in Tanzania and a beach vacation in Zanzibar?

Absolutely! Many visitors choose to combine a safari in Tanzania with a beach vacation in Zanzibar. After an exciting wildlife adventure, you can relax and unwind on the pristine beaches of Zanzibar, indulge in water sports, explore the historic Stone Town, and enjoy the rich cultural heritage of the island.

4.What Wildlife can I expect to see on a Safari?

Tanzania is home to a wide array of wildlife. On safaris, you have the opportunity to spot the Big Five (elephant, lion, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros), as well as other animals such as giraffes, zebras, wildebeests, cheetahs, hippos, crocodiles, and various antelope species. The Serengeti is renowned for its annual wildebeest migration, considered one of the most impressive wildlife spectacles in the world.

5.Do I need visa to visit Tanzania and Zanzibar?

Yes, most visitors to Tanzania and Zanzibar require a visa. The type of visa and the application process may vary based on your nationality and the duration of your stay. It's recommended to check with the Tanzanian embassies or consulates in your country or consult with a travel agent to ensure you have the necessary visa documentation before your trip.

6.Are Safaris and Beach activities in Zanzibar suitable for families?

Yes, both safaris in Tanzania and beach activities in Zanzibar can be enjoyable for families. There are family-friendly lodges and resorts that cater to the needs of children, and safari guides are experienced in providing educational and engaging experiences for young visitors. Zanzibar's beaches offer safe swimming areas and various water activities suitable for all ages.
